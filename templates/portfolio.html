Inconvenient Questions Mobile App

In the Fall of 2014 while I was studying User Centered Design at the National University of Singapore, my teammates and I designed a prototype of a mobile application for Inconvenient Questions (IQ). IQ is a sociopolitical site based in Singapore. The mission of this site is to provide a safe avenue for "netizens" to discuss controversial issues, stay informed and get answers from the experts. As the team leader, I presented our research and designs to our client, IQ.

Facebook Privacy Page Re-Design

In Spring 2014 while studying Privacy and Technology at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, I was able to conduct research on userâ€™s behavior with their privacy settings on Facebook. Through questionnaires, I found that the terminology and design strategies Facebook used at the time, left individuals confused about their own rights to their privacy. While legally compiling a plethora of personal data, Facebook lacked in practicing a comprehensive design strategy that users could easily navigate through. From research gathered, I began designing a more intuitive Privacy Settings section. I found that 9 out of 10 subjects preferred my design over the original in the areas of terminology, transparency of information, and ease of use.
